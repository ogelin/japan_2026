Getting AroundTransit & IC Card

🪙 Suica / IC Card — Do This First

  • Get it at Narita Airport on April 2 — JR East Welcome Suica machines near arrivals
  • Works on almost all trains, buses, and subways in Tokyo, Hiroshima & Kyoto
  • Also works at convenience stores, vending machines, and many restaurants
  • Load ¥3,000–5,000 to start — top up at any train station machine
  • Can also add to Apple Wallet / Google Pay if you prefer contactless via phone
  • Keep it in an accessible pocket — you'll tap it dozens of times a day

🚄 Shinkansen (Bullet Train)

  • Tokyo → Hiroshima: ~4 hrs on the Nozomi (Apr 8). Checkout by 10 AM.
  • Hiroshima → Kyoto: ~1.5 hrs on the Nozomi (Apr 11)
  • Kyoto → Tokyo: ~2.25 hrs (Apr 14 — allow 4+ hrs before your Narita flight)
  • Book Shinkansen tickets in advance — JR Pass or reserve at station
  • Nozomi is fastest but JR Pass holders note: Nozomi requires extra reservation
  • Luggage forwarding (takuhaibin) from hotel to hotel is affordable and worth it on transit days

🚇 Tokyo Subway Tips

  • Suica covers JR + Tokyo Metro + Toei lines — no need to buy separate tickets
  • Kyoto to Nijō Castle: Karasuma Line → Karasuma Oike → Tozai Line → Nijojo-mae (~15 min from apartment)
  • Google Maps is excellent for Tokyo transit — use it freely
  • Trains run ~5 AM to midnight. Last trains matter for Golden-Gai nights!
  • Priority seats (silver seats) — offer them to elderly passengers

⛴️ Miyajima Ferry

  • Take the JR Ferry from Miyajimaguchi — covered by JR Pass
  • ~10 minute crossing. Ferries run frequently.
  • Leave Hiroshima by 7:30 AM to catch an early ferry and beat the crowds
  • The deer on the island roam freely — keep food in your bag!
  • Suica works for the ferry if you're not using JR Pass

What to BringPacking Notes

Footwear & Walking

  • Comfortable walking shoes — you will easily do 15,000–25,000 steps/day
  • Slip-on shoes or easy-remove shoes for temples (you'll take them off constantly)
  • Blister plasters / moleskin — non-negotiable for week 2
  • Compression socks for the long Shinkansen days

Weather — Early April

  • Highs ~16–20°C, lows ~8–12°C — layer up
  • Light jacket or cardigan essential for evenings
  • Compact umbrella — April showers are common
  • Sunscreen — spring sun can be deceptive
  • Comfortable layers for temple-hopping days

Tech & Practical

  • Pocket WiFi or SIM card — book before you go
  • Portable power bank — long days drain phones fast
  • Universal adapter (Japan uses Type A — same as Canada, you're fine)
  • Small day bag / tote for flea market finds 🛍️
  • Earbuds for Shinkansen rides

Thrift & Shopping Days

  • Leave space in your luggage — or plan to ship finds home
  • Yamato Transport (Kuroneko) ships packages internationally from Japan
  • Bring a reusable tote — most shops charge for bags
  • Cash (yen) for flea markets — many vendors don't take cards
  • Small bills — markets prefer exact change

Etiquette Reminders

  • No eating while walking (except at Miyajima street food stalls)
  • Bow slightly when thanking shop staff
  • Both hands when giving/receiving business cards or gifts
  • Keep voices low on trains and in temples
  • Trash cans are rare — carry a small bag for wrappers

Health & Wellness

  • Jet lag: try to stay awake until 10 PM on April 2
  • Hydration — konbini (convenience stores) are everywhere for water
  • Any prescriptions in original packaging with documentation
  • Basic medication — Japanese pharmacies are great but language barrier
  • Hand sanitizer — temples have water basins but it's nice to have
